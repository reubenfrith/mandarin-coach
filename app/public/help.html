Mandarin Coach · 汉语教练

How to use your coach

A personal Mandarin tutor that checks your Chinese, explains why a mistake is wrong in plain English, and remembers the errors you keep making so it can drill you on exactly those.

What it is

Most tools mark an answer wrong and move on. Mandarin Coach diagnoses the root cause, grounds the explanation in a real grammar rule, and logs the mistake to your private error history. It's built for self-directed learners around the HSK 2–4 plateau — people who can already hold a slow conversation but keep tripping on the same handful of patterns.

What is HSK?汉语水平考试

HSK (汉语水平考试, Hànyǔ Shuǐpíng Kǎoshì) is the standard scale for Chinese proficiency, from Level 1 (beginner) to Level 6 (advanced). It's a handy shorthand for "how much Chinese do I know." This coach uses the classic HSK 1–6 standard and knows all ~5,000 words in it.

You don't need to know your exact level. When you first sign in, the coach asks you to pick the closest band (HSK 1–2, 3–4, or 5–6) and your biggest struggle, then adapts from there. Look up any word and it tells you that word's HSK level too.

What you can do

Check a sentence

Type any Chinese sentence. The coach spots the error, gives the corrected version, and explains the underlying rule — not just "wrong," but why.

Try: 她把书被他借走了

Ask a grammar question

Ask about anything that confuses you and get a plain-English explanation with examples. It pulls the actual rule from its grammar library (and real-world usage from the web when useful).

Try: “What's the difference between 了 and 过?”

Look up a word

Get the pinyin, meaning, HSK level, and example sentences for any word — checked against a dictionary, so the tones and level are correct, never guessed.

Try: “What does 顺便 mean?”

“Drill me” — your targeted practice

The signature feature. Ask to be drilled and the coach builds 3–5 exercises aimed at your actual weak spots — a specific pattern, tones, or whatever you keep getting wrong — with answers included.

Try: “Drill me on my weak points”  ·  “Drill me on 把”

“What am I getting wrong?”

A longitudinal read of your mistakes: which error categories come up most, and whether each is improving or still persisting — so you know where to focus next.

Try: “What am I getting wrong?”

The four buttons on the welcome screen — Check this sentence, 了 vs 过, Drill me on 把, and What am I getting wrong? — are quick shortcuts to these.

How it remembers you

Every correction is saved to your own private error log. Over time the coach can say things like “this is your 4th 把-structure error” and show whether it's getting better or worse. That longitudinal memory — the thing a once-a-week tutor or a generic app can't give you — is the whole point. Your history is tied to your login and kept separate from everyone else's.

Tips

  • Switch between light and dark with the ☀️ / 🌙 button in the top-right header.
  • Be specific — paste the exact sentence you're unsure about rather than describing it.
  • Ask “why?” The coach is built to explain root causes, not just hand you the fix.
  • Write in Chinese or English — it reads both, and replies in English with Chinese examples.
  • Come back regularly. The more you use it, the sharper its picture of your weak spots becomes.
